About Me

My name is Cyrus Kuhn, and I am a student at Bucknell University. During my time at Bucknell, I have had countless opportunities to explore different fields of study, partake in different activities, and meet many completely different people.

My strongest value is that of respect of others, which has been crucial in helping me form healthy relationships as well as establishing and reinforcing functioning communities. Whenever I am given respect from someone else, I simply feel good, and it allows me to work to the best of my ability, even when problems outside of the workplace are lurking behind. As such, I do my best to treat everyone with respect, even if I am not always treated the same way. The more people that you are kind to, the more people you will find that are kind to you.

We live in a world full of many hateful people, and it can be easy to become angry and breed more hate. Love one another. As someone with an older sister, there have been some days where I did not like her. But no matter what, I will always love her. There will be many people throughout your journey who upset you. You do not have to like them, but you do need to love them.

Research

My research interests are rather widespread. Throughout my life, I have had an intellectual fascination with education, and much of my research has been about education in a computer science setting. On the other hand, I am fascinated with computers on the transistor and atomic levels, and I am currently on a project researching different structures for transistors that are more effective at smaller sizes than are available today. I am also fascinated by how systems work, and I would be excited to expand my research portfolio into data structures and algorithms or ASIC design.

Projects

Programming and Engineering Projects

Kardavus

Kardavus is a project for implementing many procedural generation techniques into creating a simulated environment similar to the videogame Spore. Ultimately, the simulation will be able to demonstrate the evolution of civilizations across the Kardashev scale.

Atomic Transistor Simulation

This simulation will be used to test different transistor structures and materials using analytical and numerical analysis with fundamental physics such as quantum mechanics and molecular dynamics. Ideally, this project will be useful outside of transistor applications, though the initial scope will be less broad.
